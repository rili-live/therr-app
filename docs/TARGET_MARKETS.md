# Therr App — Target Markets

**Last Updated:** March 24, 2026
**Scope:** Core Therr App only. Niche apps (HABITS, TEEM) define their own market segments in their respective `PROJECT_BRIEF.md` files.
**Review Cadence:** Reassess quarterly or after any major product pivot.

---

## Purpose

This document defines the primary target markets for the Therr App platform — both consumer users and business users. It serves as a single source of truth for product decisions, marketing strategy, and AI-assisted development context. When building features, prioritize the needs of primary segments first.

---

## Consumer (User) Target Markets

### Primary: Health & Wellness Enthusiasts (Ages 25–40)

**Who they are:** People actively investing in their physical and mental well-being — gym-goers, runners, yoga practitioners, healthy eaters, mindfulness practitioners.

**Why they matter:**
- Naturally drawn to check-in rewards and local discovery (gyms, studios, healthy restaurants, wellness spots)
- High smartphone adoption and comfort with location sharing
- Socially oriented — fitness communities share recommendations organically
- Willing to explore new apps that enhance their routines
- High overlap with local business engagement (boutique fitness, health food, wellness services)

**How Therr serves them:**
- Discover nearby wellness spots through proximity-gated content from friends
- Earn rewards for check-ins at gyms, studios, and health-focused businesses
- Connect with like-minded people in their neighborhood via Match Up
- Find friend-recommended spots only when physically nearby (treasure-hunt discovery)

**Acquisition strategy:** Viral loops through friend recommendations at shared wellness locations; partnerships with local fitness studios for check-in rewards.

---

### Secondary: College Students & Young Professionals (Ages 18–28)

**Who they are:** Digitally native, socially active people exploring their cities — students, recent grads, young professionals in urban areas.

**Why they matter:**
- Most receptive demographic to new social apps and novel interaction models
- Actively exploring local areas (new to a city, seeking social connections)
- Tight friend groups are perfect for proximity-based features
- "Treasure hunt" content discovery appeals strongly to this age group
- Word-of-mouth adoption (zero-marketing constraint met naturally)
- Budget-conscious = highly responsive to check-in rewards and local deals

**How Therr serves them:**
- Discover what friends are doing nearby without broadcasting locations publicly
- Find deals and promotions at local businesses through geo-fenced rewards
- Build local social circles in new cities through proximity matching
- Share location-tied content that friends discover organically

**Acquisition strategy:** Campus-by-campus and neighborhood-by-neighborhood organic growth; friend invite mechanics; local deal partnerships near universities and co-working spaces.

---

### Tertiary: Local Community Advocates (Ages 30–50)

**Who they are:** People who are already active in their neighborhoods — attending local events, supporting small businesses, participating in community groups.

**Why they matter:**
- Already behave the way Therr incentivizes (local engagement, in-person connections)
- Higher spending power = more valuable to business users on the platform
- Natural evangelists for "support local" messaging
- Less price-sensitive, more relationship-driven
- Often influential in their communities (word-of-mouth amplifiers)

**How Therr serves them:**
- Discover neighborhood businesses and events through trusted friend content
- Strengthen existing local connections through proximity-based social features
- Support local businesses with check-ins and authentic engagement
- Find community events and activities happening nearby

**Acquisition strategy:** Leverage local business partnerships; community event integrations; "support local" positioning resonates strongly with this segment.

## Business User Target Markets

### Primary: Independent Local Businesses (Food, Fitness, Retail)

**Who they are:** Owner-operated restaurants, cafes, coffee shops, boutique fitness studios, yoga studios, independent retail shops, specialty stores.

**Why they matter:**
- Most underserved by large social platforms (can't compete with big-brand ad budgets)
- Highest need for foot traffic solutions — their survival depends on local customers
- Geo-fenced promotions directly solve their core problem (getting people in the door)
- Low barrier to entry: free digital storefront, optional promoted content
- Willing to experiment with new platforms if they see foot traffic results

**What Therr offers them:**
- Claim a free digital storefront visible to nearby users
- Create geo-fenced promotions that reach people within walking/driving distance
- Check-in rewards that incentivize repeat visits
- Authentic local engagement (not algorithm-gated like Instagram/Facebook)
- Analytics on foot traffic and customer engagement

**Revenue model:** Freemium storefront (free) → promoted content and premium visibility (paid). Value proposition is clear and measurable (foot traffic).

---

### Secondary: Event Venues & Experience Providers

**Who they are:** Bars, music venues, comedy clubs, escape rooms, activity centers, pop-up experiences, community event spaces.

**Why they matter:**
- Events are inherently location-based and time-bound — perfect fit for proximity-gated content
- High urgency ("happening now/tonight") drives immediate engagement
- Check-in rewards create repeat visits and loyalty
- Event-goers are naturally social and share experiences with friends
- Strong overlap with secondary consumer segment (young professionals)

**What Therr offers them:**
- Promote events to people who are physically nearby (high conversion potential)
- Proximity-gated teasers that build anticipation (discover event content when near the venue)
- Check-in rewards for repeat attendees
- Social amplification — attendees' friends discover the venue through proximity content

---

### Tertiary: Local Service Providers

**Who they are:** Hair salons, barbershops, nail studios, auto repair shops, pet groomers, tutoring centers, wellness practitioners (massage, acupuncture, chiropractic).

**Why they matter:**
- Rely almost entirely on local clientele and word-of-mouth
- Can't effectively compete on large advertising platforms
- Loyalty and repeat business are critical to their model
- Personal recommendations carry enormous weight in these categories

**What Therr offers them:**
- Local discovery through friend recommendations (proximity-gated)
- Check-in rewards that incentivize client loyalty and repeat visits
- Digital storefront for local visibility without social media marketing expertise
- Word-of-mouth amplification through the social layer

## Key Assumptions & Validation Signals

These target market definitions are based on the app's current feature set and positioning. Reassess if:

- **Product pivot**: Core features change significantly (e.g., moving away from proximity-gated content)
- **Market signal**: A different demographic shows unexpectedly strong adoption
- **Competitive shift**: A major competitor enters the local social space
- **Geographic expansion**: International markets may have different segment priorities
- **Monetization learning**: Business user segments may re-prioritize based on which verticals convert best

**Validation metrics to watch:**
- User retention by demographic segment
- Viral coefficient by segment (which users invite the most friends?)
- Business user conversion by vertical (which business types claim storefronts?)
- Check-in frequency by segment (who engages most with proximity features?)
- Revenue per business user by vertical
